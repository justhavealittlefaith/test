  • Mission

    • To relocate film industry's ecosystem to the internet
    • To reduce the transaction cost that physical cinemas incurred

  • Problems

    • Transaction cost
      • For those costs associated to run a cinema
      • Movie publishers can receive the actual amount of box office just as little as 40%; they do not reap as much as what they sow
    • Pirated movies
      • People nowadays go to cinemas less frequently and this impedes the box office directly, especially in Hong Kong and China
      • This is because the first-run movies could be downloaded very easily; or even you do not need to download but just go to video streaming website, for example Youku, LETV, etc, you can already watch a new movie without paying any fees
      • Therefore if I just need to wait for several weeks after the new movie released in cinemas and I can watch free, then why would I still go to cinema and pay for the tickets?

  • Goal

    • Targeted at the whole world for the sake of placing all new movies in our platform
    • To weed out the physical cinemas as their existence increases a lot of transaction cost, which could otherwise raise the real income of film industry

How It Works

  • When movie publishers release new movie in our platform, users/ audiences can enjoy the movie by paying $1 at the very beginning
  • Apart from this fees revenue, we will also derive advertising revenue. And these two are going to make up a movie's box office
  • And we would take part of box office as our commission
  • Of course you might concern whether the online format of box office is enough to get an amount equal to or higher than the traditional box office?

Of course you might concern whether the online format of box office is enough to get an amount equal to or higher than the traditional box office?

If traditional box office = $10 million, can online box office >= $10 million?

  • So this is the question about the difference between nominal box office and real box office
  • For the traditional format of box office, let’s say the box office is $10 million (which I treat it as nominal box office), normally the publishers or producers can take only 40-50% of the nominal box office (i.e. $5 million which I treat it as real box office)
  • But for our online format of box office, they can receive up to 80-90% of the nominal box office
  • In the long term, the online format of movie release can definitely raise the real income of film industry
